# PaintTalk.com Owner-Operator Insights — Software & Processes
**Date:** 2026-05-17  
**Source:** PaintTalk Professional Painting Contractors Forum threads and community consensus

## What Real Painters Actually Use

### Estimating & Proposals
- **Spreadsheet + QuickBooks** is still the baseline for 1-man shops. Many veterans say "If it ain't broke..."
- **PaintScout** gets consistent praise for speed and professional look. "5-minute estimates" resonates.
- **QuickBooks estimates** used for simplicity, but lacks production rates.
- **Houzz Pro** mentioned for templated proposals.
- **Pen & paper** still common for small jobs; photo of handwritten estimate texted to client.

### CRM / Business Management
- **Jobber** is the most named. Painters like scheduling + invoicing in one place. Complaint: price increases, mobile app clunky.
- **Housecall Pro** second. Liked for automated reminders and card on file. Disliked for aggressive upsells.
- **DripJobs** gaining traction for follow-up automation. Owners say "I was losing 30% of leads to no follow-up. Drips fixed it."
- **No CRM** is common. Gmail labels + calendar + memory.
- **Pipeline CRM** mentioned as PaintScout-friendly.

### Job Costing & Accounting
- **QuickBooks Online/Desktop** is the standard. Almost universal.
- Most do job costing *after the fact* in QB, not real-time.
- **Knowify** mentioned for real-time job costing integrated with QB.
- Many admit they don't track true job costs: "I know if I made money when I see bank account."
- Paint store accounts (Sherwin, PPG) statements used as proxy for material costs.

### Scheduling
- **Google Calendar** dominates solopreneurs.
- Whiteboard in shop for crews.
- Jobber/HCP calendar used if they're already paying for it.

### Lead Generation Process
1. Google Business Profile + reviews = #1 source
2. Referrals from past clients / realtors
3. Yard signs on job sites
4. Facebook community groups
5. HomeAdvisor/Angi — mixed reviews, "race to bottom"

### Sales Process (Common Thread)
- Phone call → ballpark range → on-site measure → estimate sent same day
- Follow-up 2-3 times max, then move on
- Good/Better/Best options increase close rate
- Deposit 30-50% before start, final on completion
- Text > email for communication

### Pain Points Repeated on PaintTalk
- **Follow-up is where money is lost.** "I was great at painting, terrible at sales follow-up."
- **Estimating takes too long.** Want faster, not necessarily more accurate.
- **Getting paid.** Chasing final checks. Want card on file.
- **Knowing job profitability.** Most guess. Want simple job costing without accountant.
- **Too many apps.** "I pay for Jobber, QuickBooks, and still use spreadsheets."
- **No-shows and reschedules** kill schedule.

### Processes Owners Swear By
- **Production rates:** Track sq ft / hour per painter to price accurately. Many keep personal rate sheet in Notes app.
- **Daily log:** Photo of job progress + hours worked, texted to owner.
- **Material ordering:** Call paint store, deliver to job. Track by job number in store account.
- **End-of-job walkthrough** with client + get review request immediately.
- **Friday admin block:** Invoices, follow-ups, schedule next week.

### Software Wishlist from Forum Posts
- One app that does estimate → schedule → invoice → job costing
- Mobile-first, works with gloves on
- Offline mode for basements/no signal
- Simple job costing, not construction ERP
- Automated follow-up texts
- QuickBooks sync without double entry
- Affordable: $50-100/mo max for solo

### Quotes Paraphrased from PaintTalk
- "I switched to Jobber for scheduling, but still do estimates in Excel because it's faster."
- "PaintScout made me look professional. Close rate went up 20%."
- "DripJobs follow-ups booked me 3 jobs I would have lost."
- "I don't need ServiceTitan. I'm not running an empire."
- "If I could see profit per job in real time, I'd raise prices tomorrow."

## Implications for PaintFlow MVP & Backlog

**Validated P0s:**
- Fast Good/Better/Best estimating with production rates
- Mobile job costing: hours + receipt photo
- Automated follow-up drips
- Stripe card on file + deposits
- QuickBooks Online sync

**Deprioritize for MVP:**
- Complex crew scheduling / dispatch
- Route optimization
- Advanced reporting dashboards
- Inventory management

**Backlog Priorities Adjusted Based on PaintTalk:**
1. **P0:** Estimating speed > accuracy perfection. Templates win.
2. **P0:** Job costing must be *simple* - hours + materials, not full accounting.
3. **P1:** Follow-up automation is revenue lever. Move up from P1 to P0 if possible.
4. **P1:** Text communication inside app (painters live in SMS)
5. **P2:** Review requests immediately after job close
6. **P2:** QuickBooks sync - non-negotiable for real users

**Messaging:** "Built by painters, not software companies." Emphasize one app replacing 4 subscriptions.
